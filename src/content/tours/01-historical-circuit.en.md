Journey through centuries in a single day. This flagship circuit takes you from the Mechouar Palace to the majestic ruins of Mansourah, passing through the Great Mosque, the sacred heights of El Eubbed, and the breathtaking depths of the Cave of Beni Add. Each site reveals a layer of Tlemcen’s complex history — capital of the Zianids, crossroads of civilizations, guardian of an architectural heritage unique in the world.

Walking through the gates of the Mechouar feels like stepping into the Zianid era. It’s not just about the restored walls; it’s about the play of light on the intricate zellige tilework and the quiet dignity of the royal gardens. Then comes the Great Mosque — the beating heart of the city’s spiritual and architectural history. Standing beneath its Almoravid arches, you feel the weight of centuries of devotion.

From the city center, the journey ascends to the historical complex of El Eubbed. Here, the exquisite Marinid architecture of the mosque and madrasa frames the final resting place of Sidi Boumediene, the patron saint of Tlemcen. It is a place of profound serenity, where spiritual legacy meets panoramic views of the valley.

The exploration then ventures deeper into the region's natural heritage at the Cave of Beni Add. Deep underground, this stunning natural wonder captivates with its dramatic stalactites and stalagmites, sculpted over millennia—a fascinating contrast to the man-made monuments above.

The circuit ends at the Mansourah ruins, where the solitary 14th-century minaret stands as testament to human ambition and the passage of time. The perfect spot to discuss the great medieval sieges, with views over the vast olive groves below.
